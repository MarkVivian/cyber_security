                        MOVEMENT COMMANDS
                    =======================
        MOVING WITHIN A FILE
    ---------------------------
h :
    - move left.


j : 
    - move down.


k :
    - move up.


l : 
    - move right.


w : 
    - move to the next word.


b :
    - move to the beginning of the previous word.


e : 
    - move to the end of the current word.


0 :
    - move to the beginning of the line.


$ :
    - move to the end of the line.


gg:
    - move to the top of the file.

G:
    - move to the bottom of the file.
    - You can specify a line number before G to move to that specific line.
        EG.
            press 10G to move to the 10th line of the file.


            EDITING WITHIN A FILE.
        -------------------------------
1. To select a word in visual mode:
    EG.
        press v to enter visual mode
                THEN
        press w to select the next word
                OR
        press b to select the previous word
                OR
        press e to select to the end of the current word
                OR
        press 0 to select to the beginning of the line
                OR
        press $ to select to the end of the line
                OR
        press gg to select from the current position to the top of the file
                OR
        press G to select from the current position to the bottom of the file   

            > To exit visual mode, you can press the ESC key.
                 EG.
                    press ESC while in visual mode to exit back to normal mode.


2. To select a block of text in visual mode:
    EG.
        press Ctrl + v to enter visual block mode
                THEN
        use the arrow keys to select a block of text across multiple lines and columns.


3. To select an entire line in visual mode:
    EG.
        press V to enter visual line mode
                THEN
        use the arrow keys to select multiple lines of text.


4. To copy and paste text:
    > In visual mode:
        EG.
            # copy
            press y while the text is selected to copy it to the clipboard.
                    OR
            press d while the text is selected to cut it (copy and delete) to the clipboard.

            # paste
            press p to paste the copied text after the cursor
                    OR
            press P to paste the copied text before the cursor.      
            
    > In normal mode:
        EG.
            # copy
            press yy to copy the current line
                    OR
            press yw to copy from the cursor to the end of the current word
                    OR
            press yb to copy from the cursor to the beginning of the previous word
                    OR
            press y$ to copy from the cursor to the end of the line
                    OR
            press y0 to copy from the cursor to the beginning of the line
                    OR
            press ygg to copy from the cursor to the top of the file
                    OR
            press yG to copy from the cursor to the bottom of the file
                    OR
            press 2yy to copy two lines (or any number of lines by changing the number).
                    OR
            press y^ to copy from the cursor to the beginning of the line
                    OR
            press y} to copy from the cursor to the end of the paragraph

            # paste
            press p to paste the copied text after the cursor
                    OR
            press P to paste the copied text before the cursor.


5. To delete text:
    i. in visual mode:
        EG.
            press d while the text is selected to delete it.

    ii. in normal mode:
        EG.
            press dd to delete the current line
                    OR
            press dw to delete from the cursor to the end of the current word
                    OR
            press db to delete from the cursor to the beginning of the previous word
                    OR
            press d$ to delete from the cursor to the end of the line
                    OR
            press d0 to delete from the cursor to the beginning of the line        
                    OR 
            press dgg to delete from the cursor to the top of the file
                    OR
            press dG to delete from the cursor to the bottom of the file
                    OR 
            press 2dd to delete two lines (or any number of lines by changing the number).
                    OR 
            press x to delete the character under the cursor. 
                    OR
            press X to delete the character before the cursor.


6. To undo and redo changes:
    EG.
        press u to undo the last change
                OR
        press Ctrl + r to redo the last undone change.


            MOVEMENT BETWEEN FILES, WINDOWS AND TABS 
        --------------------------------------------
1. Navigating with a sidebar:
    EG.
        press :Lexplore to open the file explorer in a vertical split on the left side of the window
                OR
        press :Vexplore to open the file explorer in a vertical split on the right side of the window
                OR
        press :Texplore to open the file explorer in a horizontal split at the bottom of the window

            > To move between the file explorer and the main editing area, you can use the Ctrl-w w command, which cycles through the open windows in Vim.
                EG.
                    press Ctrl + w
                         THEN
                    press w again to switch between the file explorer and the main editing area.

            > To close the file explorer, you can use the :q command while the file explorer window is active.
                EG.
                    press :q while in the file explorer to close it.


2. To switch between open files (buffers):
    EG.
        press :bnext to switch to the next buffer
                OR
        press :bprev to switch to the previous buffer
                OR
        press :buffer filename to switch to a specific buffer by name
                OR
        press :ls to list all open buffers and their numbers, then press :buffer # to switch to a buffer by its number.


3. To create and switch between windows (splits):
    EG.
        press :new to open a new window with an empty buffer
                OR
        press :new filename to open a new window with the specified file
                OR
        press :vnew to open a new vertical split window with an empty buffer
                OR
        press :vnew filename to open a new vertical split window with the specified file
                OR
        press :split to open a new horizontal split window with an empty buffer
                OR
        press :split filename to open a new horizontal split window with the specified file

        > To switch between open windows (splits):
            EG.
                press Ctrl + w followed by h to move to the left window
                        OR
                press Ctrl + w followed by j to move to the window below
                        OR  
                press Ctrl + w followed by k to move to the window above
                        OR
                press Ctrl + w followed by l to move to the right window

        > To close the current window, you can use the :q command.
            EG.
                press :q while in the window you want to close.


4. To create and switch between tabs:
    EG.
        press :tabnew to open a new tab with an empty buffer
                OR
        press :tabnew filename to open a new tab with the specified file

        > To switch between open tabs:
            EG.
                press gt to go to the next tab
                        OR
                press gT to go to the previous tab
                        OR
                press :tabn to go to the next tab
                        OR
                press :tabp to go to the previous tab
                        OR
                press :tabfirst to go to the first tab
                        OR
                press :tablast to go to the last tab

        > To close the current tab, you can use the :tabclose command.
            EG.
                press :tabclose while in the tab you want to close.


            SEARCHING AND REPLACE TEXT.
        --------------------------------
1. To search for text:
    EG.
        press / followed by the search term to search forward in the file
            * Note: search forward means searching from the current cursor position towards the end of the file.
                OR
        press ? followed by the search term to search backward in the file
            * Note: search backward means searching from the current cursor position towards the beginning of the file.
                OR
        press * to search for the word under the cursor forward
                OR
        press # to search for the word under the cursor backward    
                OR
        press g* to search for the word under the cursor forward as a substring
                OR
        press g# to search for the word under the cursor backward as a substring

                > After searching, pressing n will go to the next occurrence of the search term in the same direction as the original search.
                    EG.
                        press n after searching forward to go to the next occurrence forward
                                OR
                        press n after searching backward to go to the next occurrence backward


2. To replace text:
    EG.
        press :s/old/new to replace the first occurrence of "old" with "new" in the current line
                OR
        press :s/old/new/g to replace all occurrences of "old" with "new" in the current line
                OR
        press :%s/old/new/g to replace all occurrences of "old" with "new" in the entire file
                OR
        press :%s/old/new/gc to replace all occurrences of "old" with "new" in the entire file with confirmation before each replacement
                OR
        press :s/old/new/gc to replace all occurrences of "old" with "new" in the current line with confirmation before each replacement
                OR
        press :%s/old/new/gi to replace all occurrences of "old" with "new" in the entire file, ignoring case
                OR
        press :s/old/new/gi to replace all occurrences of "old" with "new" in the current line, ignoring case
                OR
        press :%s/\<old\>/new/g to replace only whole word occurrences of "old" with "new" in the entire file
                OR
        press :s/\<old\>/new/g to replace only whole word occurrences of "old" with "new" in the current line
                OR
        press :%s/old/new/gc to replace all occurrences of "old" with "new" in the entire file with confirmation before each replacement, ignoring case
                OR
        press :s/old/new/gc to replace all occurrences of "old" with "new" in the current line with confirmation before each replacement, ignoring case
